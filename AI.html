WELCOME TO AI WEBPAGE

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE (AI) is the capability of computational systems to perform tasks typically associated with human intelligence, as learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and decision-making. It is a field of research in engineering, mathematics and computer science that develops and studies methods and software that enable machines to perceive their environment and use learning and intelligence to take actions that maximize their chances of achieving defined goals

THE FOUNDATION OF AI(1940-1956)

  • 1950: British mathematician Alan Turing published the paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," posing the famous question: "Can machines think?" He introduced the Turing Test as a way to measure machine intelligence.
  • 1956: The term "Artificial Intelligence" was officially coined by computer scientist John McCarthy at the Dartmouth Summer Research Project. This conference is widely considered the birthplace of AI as an independent field of study.

KEY USES OF AI

  1. Healthcare: AI assists in medical imaging (detecting diseases like cancer from X-rays or MRIs), drug discovery (predicting protein structures and testing chemical compounds), and personalized patient care.

  2. Business & Finance: AI is used for fraud detection in real-time, algorithmic trading, automated customer service (chatbots), and managing complex administrative workflows.

  3. Cybersecurity: systems monitor networks 24/7 to identify anomalies, detect malware, and respond to threats faster than human analysts could.

  4. Innovation: By simulating scenarios and analyzing variables,AI accelerates research, leading to new technologies and breakthroughs .

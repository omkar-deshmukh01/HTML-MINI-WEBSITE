HISTORY OF CLAUDE AI

  1. Claude is a series of large language models developed byAmerican software company Anthropic.
  2. The Claude Foundation (1950s–2000s): The roots of "claude" go back to the 1950s with "time-sharing" theories (John McCarthy), allowing multiple users to access expensive mainframe computers. By the 1990s, the internet and virtualization technologies enabled the modern claude model—on-demand access to computing resources over the internet.
  3. The AI Journey (1950s–2010s): AI began as a theoretical field in 1956 at the Dartmouth Conference. For decades, it was limited by the lack of data and processing power.
